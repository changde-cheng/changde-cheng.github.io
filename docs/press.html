Publicity/Press

Dr. Changde Cheng’s achievements and scientific discoveries have been recognized and reported in professional journals and news media across America, Europe, and Asia, in multiple languages.

  1. 2023 April: Cheng speaks at the Evolvability conference organized by the Center of Advanced Study (CAS) at the Norwegian Academy of Science and Letters.
  2. 2022: Our single-cell RNA-seq analysis method was rated highly by researchers at the University of Sydney in Genome Biology .
  3. 2022: Our work in aging and genetics is featured in a popular undergraduate textbook - Evolution.
  4. 2021: Encouragement from Stanford professor of biology Dmitri Petrov - “A fascinating paper that passes my ’so jealous - why didn’t I think of this?’ test by a big margin.”
  5. 2021: Professor Thomas Flatt from the University of Fribourg in Switzerland, recommended our research on aging and molecular evolution (Faculty of 1000).
  6. 2020: Our work on sex-specific selection and transcriptome was in the top 10% most cited in PLoS Genetics.
  7. 2018: Dr. Cheng’s work was highlighted by Carin Moonin in Notre Dame News - What makes a good malaria vector.
  8. 2016: Professor Deborah Charlesworth from the University of Edinburgh recommended our work on sex-specific selection and gene expression (Faculty of 1000).
  9. 2016: Our Y chromosome study was highly recommended by Seth Redmond and Daniel Neafsey from the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard - Trends in Parasitology.
  10. 2016: Our Y chromosome study was highlighted in Phys.org: “new technology to sequence mosquito sex chromosome.’’
  11. 2016: Our Y chromosome paper was highlighted in Sheng Wu Tong and Yi Du - “mosquito Y chromosome sequenced.”
  12. 2016: ``Scientists unlock genetic secret that could help fight malaria.’’ in EurekAlert!
  13. 2016: “Researchers lay groundwork for advanced disease control”, as highlighted by Sarah Craig, - ScienceDaily.
  14. 2012: Professor Nico M van Straalen from Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam recommended our ecological genomics study of chromosomal inversions - Faculty of 1000.
  15. 2012: Our work on chromosomal inversions was featured on the cover of the April issue of Genetics -Eck Institute.
  16. 2012: Professor Patrik Nosil from the University of Colorado, Boulder, recognized our ‘incidental island’ hypothesis in the book Oxford Series in Ecology and Evolution: Ecological Speciation.
  17. 2011: Mark Kirkpatrick (University of Texas at Austin) and Andrew Kern (Rutgers University) highlighed my ecological genomics research - Genetics.
  18. 2011: Paul Roepe (Georgetown University), George Dimopoulos (Imperial College London), Thomas Mitchell-Olds (Duke University), and Bao-Hua Song (the University of North Carolina at Charlotte) recommended our study on the divergence of resistance to Plasmodium - Faculty of 1000.
  19. 2010: W. Gilroy highlighted our work on immunity in emerging species of a major mosquito carrier of malaria - ScienceDaily
  20. 2010: Thomas Turner (UC Santa Barbara) and Matthew Hahn (Indiana University, Bloomington) recommended our paper on “speciation or divergent islands” - Molecular Ecology.
  21. 2010: Cheng is awarded the Genomics and Bioinformatics Fellowship at the Eck Institute for Global Health - Notre Dame News.
